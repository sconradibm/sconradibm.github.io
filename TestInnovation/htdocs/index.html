Success Stories

Pursue Your Passion for the Environment

Since she was 12, Allison Stone wanted to be on the ocean. As a hydrographic senior survey technician on board the NOAA ship Thomas Jefferson, her primary job is to make sure the ship is seeing the sea floor exactly how the sea floor is. In doing so, Allison sees things that no one has ever seen before. “We see images of wrecks that are so precise that it’s almost like the ship is coming back to life,” she says. “Very few people ever get to experience that.” See more of Allison’s story and discover how you can pursue your passion for science and the environment through a career with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration.

A Cultural Awakening Helps Launch a Million-Dollar Business

David Wiegand has big plans for his construction company, The Sparrow Group, a firm in New Mexico with $1 million in revenue that prioritizes the hiring of Native Americans. David, whose heritage includes Hopi and Navajo, hopes to keep growing his business in “The Land of Enchantment” while working on projects in Nevada and California. David appreciates how much the Minority Business Development Agency has helped him. “It’s like you have a backstage pass from behind the curtain, where nobody gets to look into government contracting. MBDA knows what’s behind that curtain,” he says. Resources from the MBDA have been pivotal in the success of The Sparrow Group. Learn more about David’s journey to business ownership and how you can help other minority-owned companies succeed through the MBDA.

Brewing Economic Success in Downtown St. Louis

Thanks to a loan from the Revolving Loan Fund program of the U.S. Economic Development Administration, residents and visitors to St. Louis can enjoy fresh hard cider made and served in an historic location. Brick River Cider Co. converted a fire department engine house built in 1892 to house the first hard ciderworks business in St. Louis. Production facilities and a small bar occupy the first floor, and the former fireman’s dormitory on the second floor has been converted to a 100-seat restaurant with full commercial kitchen. “Access to capital is always a key ingredient in any growth company,” says Russ John, owner of the Brick River Cider Co. You can help spur economic growth through a career with the EDA. Read more about the Brick River Cider Co. and how the EDA provides small businesses and entrepreneurs with the gap financing they need to start or expand their businesses.

Discover Special Interest Programs

Veterans

The Veteran Employment Council is committed to increasing the employment of non-disabled and disabled veterans throughout the Department.

Disabled

Federal regulation has removed possible barriers and increased employment opportunities for persons with disabilities, who are encouraged to apply for a job within the Department.

Internships

The Internship Program is one of several ways the Department recruits college students. By interning with one of 18 Department offices, future leaders test and develop their passion for public service.

Strategic Goals

Each of the Department’s five strategic goals advances our mission and supports this goal:

  • Accelerate American Leadership
  • Enhance Job Creation
  • Strengthen U.S. Economic and National Security
  • Fulfill Constitutional Requirements and Support Economic Activity
  • Deliver Customer-Centric Service Excellence
